About

I am a Digital Futures Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Division of Media Technology and Interaction Design at the KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden.

As a qualitative researcher and interaction designer, I focus on understanding and designing technology experiences to craft just and equitable futures. My research is situated at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Global health, taboo, marginality, and activism. With a focus on health equity, I approach technology design as a powerful catalyst for crafting pathways toward equity and empowerment for marginalized communities. I am passionate about designing interactions to nurture technology-mediated safe spaces where dialogue can thrive, especially in contexts marked by stigma and marginalization.

In my Postdoc, I am working with Madeline Balaam and Airi Lampinen to study the ethics of data collection methodologies when engaging participants on intimate and taboo topics and designing interactions that cultivate trust and a sense of collective care among various stakeholders throughout reproductive and menstrual journeys.

I completed my doctoral degree in Human-Centered Design from IIIT-Delhi, India, where I was jointly advised by Neha Kumar (GaTech) & Pushpendra Singh (IIIT-Delhi). My doctoral research, set within the urban Indian landscape, focused on uncovering how the design of menstrual technologies can effectively address cultural taboos and systemic barriers, supporting individuals throughout their menstrual journeys. I received fellowships from both the Indian Government and Google to support my doctoral research.

In addition to my academic endeavors, I am deeply involved in collaborative efforts with practitioners and non-governmental organizations in India, particularly focusing on initiatives related to menstrual health and wellbeing. I am also a trained menstrual educator and have conducted menstrual health workshops across ages and genders in India. I have contributed to developing menstrual literacy modules, structuring awareness workshops, and providing evidence for policy discourses at the state and national levels in India.

Having a foundation in computer science engineering and training in human-centered design, I frequently engage with feminist principles and community-based participatory design methods in my work. This informs my recommendations for creating socially responsible and equitable technologies, as well as approaches to system design and evaluation, and strategies for data collection and engaging with grassroots initiatives.
